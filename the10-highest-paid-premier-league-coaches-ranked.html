Soccer players make a lot of money especially from club contracts so it is needless to say that soccer players make up some of the richest people in sports. What is worth mentioning, however, is the number of money coaches and club managers receive. While years of experience and the number of trophies won can influence some coaches’ pay, the league they coach in may also be a major determinant of their salary. The premier league, being the richest league in the world and the most-watched, has one of the best coaches in history. In this list, we bring to you the top 10 richest premier league coaches of 2022, ranked by their annual salaries.

David William Moyes (£5 Million)

Via Flickr/Ambrose Carrow

With over 540 premier league appearances in his playing career and the fact that he won a championship medal, it’s no surprise that David Moyes is one of the highest-paid premier league coaches in the world. He has had several successes as a coach and has coached so many top clubs as well such as Manchester United where he won a trophy in his first game, Sunderland, and finally, West Ham United where he currently has a 3-year contract from 2021 and he currently has an annual salary of £5 million.

Frank James Lampard (£5.5 million)

Via Flickr/whateveryouwillorwanttobe

Frank James Lampard who started out his career as a footballer and even became one of Chelsea’s greatest players and one of the greatest midfielders in his generation has earned his spot on the top 10 highest-paid premier league coaches. He first started his coaching career on 31 May 2018 when he led a championship club called Derby County, and then he moved on to Chelsea in 2019, he is now in Everton and currently earns about £5 million per year.

Ralph Hasenhuttl (£6 million)

Via Flickr/Football Pictures

This former Austrian National Team player has spent so much time in the premier league that it will be no surprise he is found among the top 10 premier league coaches. He had a great football career before moving on to become a coach. He started his coaching career at SpVgg Unterhaching in October 2007, 4 years later, he went on to VfR Aalen. 2013 saw him becoming a coach for FC Ingolstadt 04, he was quite successful here before he went on to RB Leipzig and finally to Southampton from 2018 till date where he is currently earning £6 million annually.

Mikel Arteta (£8.3 million)

Via Flickr/Stuart MacFarlane

One-time Barcelona, Everton, and even Arsenal player, Mikel Arteta has had quite a career in the world of football. He started his coaching career as an assistant coach to Pep Guardiola in Manchester City, and moved on to Arsenal where he won 2 trophies, in 2020, he led Arsenal to their fourth FA cup final in seven years, and he has received two Manager of the Month awards in 2022. He earns about £8.3 million yearly

Erik Ten Hag (£9 million)

Via Flickr/Football Pictures

Despite starting his coaching career in 2012, Erik has made a name for himself already in football coaching as he is currently one of the top-paid premier league coaches in the world. He’s a one-time Bayern Munich II coach for 2 years, he has coached a number of other teams too before moving on to Manchester United in April 2022. August 2022 saw him losing his first premier league match in Manchester United which makes him the first coach since John Chapman in 1921 to lose his premier league match, and he currently earns a whooping sum of £9 million per annum.

Brandan Rodgers (£10 million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Having managed Reading, Chelsea, Swansea City, Liverpool, and Celtic, it is only normal to find Brandan Rodgers on this list. His coaching career started at the young age of 20 after a genetic knee condition forced him to retire as a player for Reading. In 2012, he took an offer to become Liverpool coach and he led them to the first runners-up position in the premier league, and 2019 saw he moved into Leicester City, and barely a year later, he won their 2021 FA cup. Brandon Rodgers currently earns about £10 million per annum.

Graham Potter (£12 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

The current coach of Chelsea will definitely find his name on this list as one of the highest-paid premier league coaches in the world. He has had quite the playing career which started in 1992 and saw him retiring in 2005. By 2008, his coaching career kicked off with Leeds Carnegie, 10 years later, he moved to Östersund, and then Swansea City before he moved on to Chelsea in September 2022 on a five-year deal and he is currently earning £12 million annually.

Antonio Conte (£15 million)

Via Flickr/ผลบอล

Antonio Conte is an Italian football manager who started his playing career in 1985 and ended in 2000. His coaching career started in 2005 and has seen him through a number of clubs including Juventus and Chelsea before moving to Tottenham Hotspur in November 2021 on an 18-month contract. He is the first Tottenham coach to go unbeaten in their first 8 league games and he has an annual earning of £15 million.

Jurgen Klopp (£16 million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Highly regarded as one of the best managers in the world, Jurgen Klopp has surely earned his flowers and his annual earnings of £16 million per annum. His coaching career started in 2001 at a club he had once played at Mainz 05, in 2008, he found his way to Borussia Dortmund and in 2015, he moved to Liverpool and a year later, he signed a 6 years deal contract with Liverpool and the club has sure seen lots of victories with Jurgen Klopp on board as their coach.

Pep Guardiola (£19 million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

The Manchester United Coach is definitely top on the list of highest-paid premier league coaches in the world, he’s also considered one of the greatest coaches of all time and he holds the record for the most consecutive won league games in La Liga, the Premier League, and the Bundesliga. This one-time Barcelona player and coach have had quite the coaching career as he has had some historic trebles, won champions league titles, and has won about 3 world cups. He is currently earning £19million per annum.
