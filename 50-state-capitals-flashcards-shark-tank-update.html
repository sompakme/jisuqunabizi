50 State Capitals Flashcards Shark Tank Update

Publish date: 2024-09-05

Welcome to our article on the 50 State Capitals Flashcards, a product that appeared on Shark Tank but did not receive a deal from the sharks. Created by Ken Bradford, these flashcards offer a unique way to learn all 50 state capitals in just 50 minutes.

Ken Bradford’s educational product, 50 State Capitals in 50 Fun Minutes, uses phonetics, cartoons, and word association to make learning the state capitals fun and easy for children. However, despite its innovative approach, the product faced challenges in the highly competitive Shark Tank environment.

Key Takeaways:

  • The 50 State Capitals Flashcards were pitched by Ken Bradford on Shark Tank but did not secure a deal.

The Pitch on Shark Tank

Ken Bradford, the founder of the 50 State Capitals Deck, made his debut on Shark Tank with an eye-catching appearance. Donning a whimsical Dr. Seuss-like coat and hat, he entered the tank ready to pitch his educational product. Ken confidently asked for a $155,000 investment, offering 10% equity in his company.

During his pitch, Ken demonstrated the power of his flashcards, showcasing their unique design and interactive features. He explained how the combination of phonetics, cartoons, and word association makes learning the 50 state capitals an engaging and enjoyable experience for children.

Despite his energetic presentation and the potential educational benefits of the product, the sharks were hesitant to invest. The primary concern raised was the lack of sales the 50 State Capitals Deck had generated thus far. Without a proven track record of consumer interest, the sharks were reluctant to commit their resources to the venture. As a result, Ken left the tank without a deal.

“The sharks were not convinced, as the product had generated virtually no sales.”

Reactions of the Sharks

During Ken Bradford’s pitch on Shark Tank, he showcased his educational product, the 50 State Capitals Deck, which aims to make learning state capitals more engaging for children. However, the sharks expressed their concerns about the lack of sales for the product.

Kevin O’Leary and Barbara Corcoran, known for their investment expertise, both decided not to invest in the 50 State Capitals Deck due to the low sales figures. Kevin Harrington and Daymond John, also experienced entrepreneurs, dropped out of the potential deal for the same reason.

“I don’t see the potential for high sales volume in this product,” expressed Kevin O’Leary during the pitch.

Ultimately, Ken Bradford left the Shark Tank without a deal. The sharks’ hesitation to invest in the educational flashcards reflects their concerns about the ability of the product to generate substantial sales.
