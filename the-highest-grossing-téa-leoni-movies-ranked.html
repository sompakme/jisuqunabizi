The Highest-Grossing Ta Leoni Movies, Ranked

Publish date: 2024-09-01

  • Ghost Town - $29.9 Million
  • Spanglish - $54.3 Million

Téa Leoni is an actress and producer who has made a name for herself in the entertainment industry. She is best known for her roles in popular films such as Deep Impact and Jurassic Park III. In addition to her successful acting career, Leoni has produced several movies and television shows, including the hit series Madam Secretary. Her talent and hard work have earned her critical acclaim and numerous accolades, including a Saturn Award for Best Actress for her role in The Family Man.

With a career of over three decades, Téa Leoni has showcased her acting prowess in various genres, from action-packed blockbusters to heartwarming comedies. Leoni’s films have collectively grossed over $1.5 billion at the box office, making her a true powerhouse in the industry.

Wyatt Earp - $25 Million

Kicking off this list is the 1994 western biopic Wyatt Earp, where Leoni portrayed Sally, the titular character's love interest. Directed by Lawrence Kasdan, the film starred Kevin Costner as the legendary lawman. Despite mixed reviews, Wyatt Earp managed to rake in $25 million at the domestic box office and was a moderate success.

Ghost Town - $29.9 Million

In the 2008 fantasy comedy Ghost Town, Leoni played Gwen, a widow entangled in a supernatural love triangle. Ricky Gervais starred as a dentist who gains the ability to see ghosts after a near-death experience. The film, directed by David Koepp, charmed audiences and critics alike, earning $29.9 million worldwide on a production budget of $20 Million, according to The Numbers.

Spanglish - $54.3 Million

Leoni took on the role of Deborah Clasky, a wealthy but neurotic housewife, in the 2004 dramedy Spanglish. The film, directed by James L. Brooks, explored the cultural and language barriers between Deborah's family and their Mexican housekeeper, played by Paz Vega. With a star-studded cast including Adam Sandler, Spanglish grossed $54.3 million globally, including an impressive $42 Million domestic collection.

The Family Man - $124.7 Million

In the 2000 romantic comedy-drama The Family Man, Leoni starred alongside Nicolas Cage as Kate Reynolds, the love interest of a high-powered businessman who gets a glimpse of the life he could have had. Directed by Brett Ratner, the film was a hit with audiences, earning $124.7 million at the box office, as reported by IMDB. Leoni's performance as Kate garnered widespread acclaim leading to her winning a Saturn Award for Best Actress.

A League Of Their Own - $132.4 Million

Téa Leoni played Racine Belles' first baseman, Racy Lacey, in the 1992 sports comedy-drama A League of Their Own. Directed by Penny Marshall, the film told the story of the All-American Girls Professional Baseball League during World War II. With an ensemble cast led by Geena Davis, Tom Hanks, and Madonna, the movie was a home run, grossing an impressive $132.4 million worldwide on a budget of just $40 Million.

Bad Boys - $141.24 Million

In the 1995 action-comedy Bad Boys, Leoni starred as Julie Mott, a witness to a murder who teams up with two Miami detectives, played by Will Smith and Martin Lawrence. The film's high-octane action and witty banter, directed by Michael Bay, propelled it to a global box-office total of $141.24 million, making it a major box-office success.

Tower Heist - $152.9 Million

Leoni played Special Agent Claire Denham in the 2011 action-comedy Tower Heist. Directed by Brett Ratner, the film follows a group of employees led by Ben Stiller who plot to steal from a wealthy businessman. With a star-studded ensemble cast, Tower Heist, on a production budget of $80 Million, climbed to $152.9 million in worldwide box office earnings, according to Box Office Mojo.

Fun With Dick And Jane - $203 Million

In the 2005 comedy Fun with Dick and Jane, Leoni starred opposite Jim Carrey as Jane Harper, a suburban wife who turns to a life of crime with her husband after they lose their jobs. Directed by Dean Parisot, the film's satirical take on corporate greed resonated with audiences, grossing a massive $203 million globally, boosted by an impressive domestic collection of $110.5 Million.

Deep Impact - $349.4 Million

Leoni took on the role of ambitious reporter Jenny Lerner in the 1998 disaster film Deep Impact. Directed by Mimi Leder, the movie followed humanity's efforts to prevent a comet from colliding with Earth. Deep Impact made a massive splash at the box office with a star-studded cast including Morgan Freeman and Elijah Wood, earning around $140.4 Million in the US and a whopping $349.4 million worldwide as per Rotten Tomatoes.

Jurassic Park III - $365.9 Million

Topping the list is the 2001 sci-fi adventure Jurassic Park III, where Leoni played Amanda Kirby, a woman stranded on an island inhabited by dinosaurs. Directed by Joe Johnston, the film is the third installment of the blockbuster Jurassic Park series and continued the legacy of the series with a successful box office run, earning over $365.9 million at the global box office.

Téa Leoni's impressive filmography showcases her versatility as an actress and her ability to captivate audiences across various genres. She has proved her mettle as a successful actress with significant roles in several big-budget flicks and starring alongside industry legends, starting from Morgan Freeman to Jim Carrey. Jurassic Park III is the highest-grossing film of Leoni’s career, with a staggering $365.9 Million haul.
