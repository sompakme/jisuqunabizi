From Dorothy Gale of The Wizard Of Oz and Scarlett O'Hara of Gone With The Wind to Emma Stone's Poor Things and Lily Gladstone's Killers Of The Flower Moon, actresses are dominating Hollywood with their striking performances.

There were several staggering performances in this year's roster of releases, but only five are now eligible for an Academy Award, having secured a nomination in the prestigious category of "Best Actress In A Leading Role."

But this time around, controversy arose when Barbie's titular star, Margot Robbie, was notably excluded from the running for one of the most prestigious awards on planet Earth despite her unforgettable character in the most successful movie of the year.

Fans the world over felt that Robbie, an Australian actress, was snubbed, ushering in a multi-tiered controversy dubbed "The Great Barbie Backlash-Backlash" by entertainment trade publication The Hollywood Reporter.

Emotions ran so strong that the Australian Police Force even jokingly claimed they would investigate whether "Margot Robbie got robbed" at the upcoming 96th Oscars.

Similar outrage surfaces nearly every year, but no one points a finger at the Oscars' "Best Actress In A Leading Role" nominees – typically because almost everyone agrees the nominated performances are phenomenal, too.

Let’s delve into this year's "Best Actress" nominees' performances, explore the details of their roles, and finally, take a look at their individual net worths.

5 Lily Gladstone: Best Actress Nominee, 'Killers Of The Flower Moon'

Lily Gladstone’s Net Worth (Estimated): $900,000

Lily Gladstone started her career in 2012; for eleven years, she strove to make a prominent name for herself.

In that decade (between 2012 and today), Gladstone was cast in many shorts and lead roles.

But in 2023, her life changed when Martin Scorsese, one of the greatest directors in the film industry today, cast her alongside Oscar winner Leonardo DiCaprio.

Gladstone's performance in Killers Of The Flower Moon earned her a "Best Actress" nomination, one of the most sought after awards in the biggest and most influential ceremony of the season.

In Killers Of The Flower Moon, she portrayed Mollie Burkhart, an activist who wanted to save her community from a spree of murders caused by oil and greed.

2 Emma Stone: Best Actress Nominee, 'Poor Things'

In Poor Things, Emma Stone played the character of Bella Baxter, who is brought back to life by an unorthodox scientist and given an infant's mind.

The film follows as Bella discovers the world's complexity through her unusual cognitive predicament, and how she copes with it.

Predicting who will bring home the most prestigious Academy Award trophy is challenging, due to the strength of the performances turned in by all the actresses nominated in this competitive category.

However, some publications are favoring Emma Stone this year, while the majority are placing their bets on Native American actress Lily Gladstone.

Surprisingly (or perhaps unsurprisingly), no indigenous performer has won an Academy Award for acting. In 2020, Wes Studi, a Native American from the Cherokee Nation, received an Honorary Oscar.

However, no Native American actor has taken home the gold in the "Best Actress" or "Best Actor" category to date, and as such, Oscar-watchers are rallying behind Lily Gladstone to make history.
